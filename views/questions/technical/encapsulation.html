What is encapsulation, and why is it important in OOP?

Encapsulation is the idea that access to the inner workings of an object should be controlled carefully. A module is considered "encapsulated" iff its implementation is completely hidden, and it can only be accessed through a documented interface.

Encapsulation is important because it helps to compartmentalize elements within a larger project, and by so doing, helps to minimize the risk of misuse of particular functionalities. For example, think of how cars work; the driver gets a steering wheel, brakes, accelerator, et cetera, and it's only through these that they control how the car moves. The car itself handles the actual acceleration and turning, because the actual inner workings of the engine are too complex for the average user. If the user were to be allowed to meddle with the innermost workings of the vehicle, they could cause all kinds of damage due to their ignorance of how the actual act of acceleration (or what have you) was supposed to be performed.
