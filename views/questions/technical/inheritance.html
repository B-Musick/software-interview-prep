What is inheritance, and why is it important in OOP?

Inheritance further extends the sort of familial metaphor in OOP, and much like the actual concept, it just means that child classes get what their parents had (with the exception that no one need die for OOP inheritance to take place, and child classes needn't fight over who gets what).

It's important to note that inheritance is for "is-a" relationships, not for "has-a" relationships. For example, the class "Bathroom" should inherit from the "Room" class (because a bathroom is a room), but a "Shower" class should NOT inherit from "Bathroom" (because though a bathroom has a shower, the statement "a shower is a bathroom" is both inaccurate and markedly insalubrious).
